About BMI (Body Mass Index)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a quick and simple method to estimate whether a person has a healthy body weight for a given height.

Formula:

BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))²

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or greater
  • Severely Obese: BMI 35 or greater

Importance of BMI:

BMI is an important tool to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Limitations of BMI:

BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, overall body composition, and differences due to race and sex.
